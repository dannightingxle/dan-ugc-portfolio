## Daily targets

- **5 IG DMs** (cold, after seeing their Stories ad)
- **2 cold emails** (to marketing leads found via LinkedIn)
- **1 duo pitch** (specifically as husband-wife)
- **1 platform brief application** (Vidzie / Influi / Insense)

That's ~9 touchpoints/day, ~45/week, ~180/month. Industry conversion ~3-5%, so realistically 5-9 brand conversations a month, 1-3 actual briefs.

## Subject line patterns that work

- `[Specific number] hook concepts for [Brand]`
- `Quick UGC idea for [Brand]`
- `Saw your [product] ad - had three thoughts`
- `[Brand] x UGC - 30-second pitch`
- `From a Form Nutrition customer` (use their actual customer angle)

Avoid: "Collaboration opportunity", "Partnership proposal", "Hi there!". Reads spammy.

## Outbound tracking sheet

Copy this header row into a Google Sheet (or use the targets.csv next to this file):

```
Brand | Niche | Website | Instagram | Marketing Lead | Email | LinkedIn | Stage | Last Touched | Reply? | Notes
```

### Stage column values

- `0. Researched` - on the list, not yet contacted
- `1. DM sent`
- `2. Email sent`
- `3. Replied`
- `4. Concept sent`
- `5. Brief agreed`
- `6. Filming`
- `7. Delivered`
- `8. Paid`
- `X. Dead` - bounced / explicit no / ghosted 3+ touches

### Workflow

1. **Monday morning**: research 25 new brands, fill rows
2. **Tuesday-Friday**: 5 DMs + 2 emails per day from the list
3. **Friday afternoon**: review replies, update stages, schedule follow-ups
4. **Monthly**: archive `X. Dead`, count won briefs, check conversion %

### Realistic conversion benchmarks (per Joshi's data + adjusted for your credibility)

- Cold DM → reply: 5-10% (most ignore)
- Cold email → reply: 8-15% (lower volume, higher signal)
- Reply → concept: 50%
- Concept → brief: 30-50%
- Net: ~2-4% of outbound becomes a paid brief

So 200 outbound → 4-8 paid briefs. At your rates (£100-£225 each + retainers), that's a £600-£1.8k month from cold alone, scaling with outbound volume. Retainers stack on top.

## What NOT to do

- Don't send identical DMs to 50 brands in a batch - they spot it instantly
- Don't follow up more than twice on a cold outreach - move on
- Don't pitch perpetuity rights or unlimited revisions
- Don't undercut your rate card just to land a brief - you're not a beginner
- Don't pitch brands you've never used/heard of - the "I'm an actual customer" angle is your unfair advantage
